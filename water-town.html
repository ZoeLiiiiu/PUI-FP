Water Towns in “Jiangnan”

Individual Work

Time: 07/2020

Duration: 2 weeks

Material: Card Board, Voile, Gouache on Paper

Water Town Model

Size: 12 x 12 x 12 in.

In this project, I extract shapes of trapezoid from the houses in Wu Guanzhong's ink wash paintings about landscapes and waterscapes in Jiangnan, an area in south China. By reconstructing the layout of the buildings and adding elements to create a sense of futuristic city, I try to display the dramatic mixture of traditions and imaginary technological cityscape.

The Conceptual Drawing of the Entire Water Town

The Conceptual Drawing of the Water Town Detail

The Conceptual Drawing of the Water Town

Size: 21 x 15 in.

Inspired by the color in Pezo von Ellrichshausen's painting, I aim to use a new approach to express the project "Jiangnan." I rearrange the structure of the “houses” and the colors, highlighting the curved stream surrounding the straight shaped elements.
