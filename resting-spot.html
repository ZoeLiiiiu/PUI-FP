The Resting Spot Experiential Drawing

The resting spot is located in the boreal forest in Canada. We started by analyzing the site condition and a precedent. We specifically analyzed the longhouse to understand how ancient people deal with the climate. Then, I started developing the resting spot by making sketch models. Due to the cold and windy climate, I made it into a shelter. After being iterated several times, the final model represents how the shelter responds to the natural environment and the spatial experience in it. The canopy faces the lake, blocking the rain and the wind from the lakeside, keeping the inside area of the shelter warm and dry, and providing hikers with a place to temporarily take a break. Moreover, the curved shape redirects the wind and the rain. The form leads people to explore the shelter. The “tail” evokes people's curiosity, inviting them to go further in, and by going further in, they will access the public and private areas.
