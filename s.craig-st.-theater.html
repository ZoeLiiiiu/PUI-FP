The Experiential Drawing

The theater is located on S. Craig St. in Oakland, Pittsburgh. I aimed to use design elements to attract visitors to view and interact with the building. The overall form of the building envelope follows the interior closely, but the gap between them becomes the main corridor. When entering the building, people first pass the building envelope, then the theatre exterior, and then the theatre interior, all connected by the same corridor. The front wall of the lobby is glass, blurring the boundary between the inside and outside of the building and inviting people to come in. Following the concept of attracting people to interact with the building, my façade is inspired by Chinese Landscape ink painting. The implicit beauty of Chinese art always invites people to explore further. People get a different experience of going further. The envelope, theater exterior, and theatre interior each have different façade designs, which are mountains, fountains, and villages respectively. It is almost like traveling through a landscape painting. Just as the ancient Chinese poem reads, “After endless mountains and rivers that leave doubt whether there is a path out, suddenly one encounters the shade of a willow, bright flowers, and a lovely village.”
