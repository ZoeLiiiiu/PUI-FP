This project explores computational representations as structured artifacts. Our design vocabulary is comprised of conic sections — basic curved geometric figures derived from a cone, and we modify the section to create a pavilion.

When creating the conic section, we kept the two cutting planes parallel so that the surface generated would have two parallel edges. Then, we created four different iterations of surfaces by adjusting the angle and distance of the cutting planes. We mirror those surfaces along their two diagonals, creating four modules. Then, we developed four ways of arranging the modules, producing sixteen variations of structure. During the process, we manually adjust each module and structure to let them fit together. We also intentionally clean the seams to make sure that the structure connected properly by sharing edges and points.

We selected one variation, further developing it by adding more details. The final structure is symmetrical. It has three levels of hierarchy ---- the supporting frame, the two solid palettes, and the transparent palette with a grid structure between them. We imagine the pavilion to be a place for the public to relax, so we emphasize the sense of cleanliness and lightness by using simple materials. The solid and transparent pallet brings the contrast between lights and shadows, adding more interest to the pavilion.
